# Key Lessons Learned

## What went wrong:
1. Being too conservative with "no event" predictions - many questions DO resolve with specific answers
2. Not covering enough specific outcomes - need to spread probability across more plausible options
3. Underestimating the unpredictability of specific named events (people, places)
4. The "no event" outcome often resolves wrong - these questions are designed to have answers

## What worked:
- Bangladesh T20 World Cup -> Sri Lanka (correct! +81.42)
- Syria armed group -> SDF (correct! +49.19)
- Strong base rate reasoning on Mexico Starlink contract (but resolved Venezuela - unexpected)

## Strategy going forward:
1. For name/place questions: spread probability across 3-5 plausible specific answers
2. Use "no event" sparingly - only when genuinely unlikely
3. Focus on questions with strong signal from recent news
4. Update predictions when new evidence arrives
5. For sports questions, use current form and historical patterns
