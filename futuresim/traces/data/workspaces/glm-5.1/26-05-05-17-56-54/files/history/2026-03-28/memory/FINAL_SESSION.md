## Key Lessons Learned

### Prediction Strategy
1. **Read questions carefully** - Misread Pakistan bomb question (NW Pakistan vs S Thailand)
2. **Include Qatar/UAE in diplomatic questions** - They appear frequently as mediators
3. **Be specific with location names** - White House vs Washington; full names like "IRIS Dena"
4. **Cast wider nets for names** - Less prominent people/players often correct
5. **Don't override strong initial predictions** with insufficient evidence
6. **Update predictions when strong evidence emerges** (Tyson Fury/Makhmudov)
7. **Consider country-specific context** - Italian rail for Italian questions

### Hard-to-Predict Categories
1. **Specific city/town names** - Often obscure towns (Sodari, Bannu, Parand, Minab, Beit Awwa)
2. **Specific people** - Less prominent figures (Balendra Shah, Menashe Zalka, Ayman Mohamad Ghazali)
3. **Creative/unexpected names** - "Project Vault", "BETS OFF Act", CPFIK
4. **Sports matchups** - Champions League draws, T20 World Cup results
5. **Military hardware** - PrSM missiles, KC-135 aircraft

### What Worked Well
1. Broad distributions for uncertain questions
2. Research-based updates for near-term questions
3. Including second/third options that weren't top picks
4. Quick updates when strong news evidence appeared
5. Sports predictions (AFCON, Australian Open)
