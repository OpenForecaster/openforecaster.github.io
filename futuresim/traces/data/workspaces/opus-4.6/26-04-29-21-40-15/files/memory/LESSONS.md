# Lessons Learned

## Critical Rules
1. READ QUESTIONS CAREFULLY - geographic specifics matter (e.g., "northern New Zealand" vs generic)
2. NEVER assign >0.5 probability unless very specific news confirms it
3. This sim has its OWN reality - background knowledge is unreliable
4. Spread probability across 5 diverse candidates when uncertain
5. Look for cross-question correlations (e.g., ICE Minnesota + DOJ Tim Walz)
6. Even "confirmed" info may be imprecise (Sulawesi vs Muna island nearby)
7. The sim often picks unexpected/lesser-known answers
8. CRITICAL: Read question text EXACTLY - "not participate in Strait of Hormuz" ≠ "not participate in World Cup"
9. Italy's "Emergency" NGO operates Mediterranean rescues - not just MSF/SOS/OpenArms
10. F1 DNS can happen to top teams (McLaren both DNS at Chinese GP)
