## Key Takeaways
- NEVER assign >0.5 probability to a single outcome unless very sure from news
- Consider BOTH genders for sports questions
- The sim world has its own reality - don't assume standard outcomes
- Spread probability more evenly when uncertain
- Better to have 0.15 on correct + 4 wrong than 0.85 on wrong
- The Texas measles answer shows that background knowledge is unreliable in this sim
- Chelsea coaching change happened but wasn't in my news database - need to search harder
- Mykhailo Fedorov was Ukraine's digital transformation minister, showing unexpected appointments

## Calibration Adjustments
- For "who/what" string questions with many possible answers, keep max at 0.25-0.30
- Only go above 0.40 if specific news article confirms the answer
- Always include 5 diverse candidates to maximize coverage
- Don't anchor too heavily on prior knowledge - this sim has its own events
